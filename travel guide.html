Brief History of Pampanga

Pampanga, one of the richest provinces in the Philippines, was reorganized as a province by the Spaniards on December 11, 1571. Located in the central part of Region III, bordered on the north by Tarlac and Nueva Ecija, and on the east by Bulacan. The terrain is relatively flat, with only one distinct mountain, Mount Arayat. Martn de Goiti, a Spanish conquistador, established this province in 1571. The name was derived from the native Kapampangan words "pangpang ilog," which means "riverside," and refers to the early Malayan settlements along the Rio Grande de la Pampanga.

Kapampangan Culture

Kapampangans are well known for their passion for food and their festivities reflect their happy life. But do you know one of the most exciting things to do in Pampanga? Festivals, indeed! Pampanga is well-known for hosting some of the country's most spectacular events. In fact, millions of people, both domestic and international, visit the region each year just to see these anticipated events. So, put on your dancing shoes and let's discover Pampanga's best festivals!

Pampanga's Natural Environment

Pampanga has a lot to offer not only in the food industry, but also in natural beauty, with stunning waterfalls, majestic mountain views, and peaceful wetlands. In the province, you can go on a hike, swim in natural pools, and see various migratory birds.
